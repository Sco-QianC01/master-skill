### 1. Rob Walling

- **One-liner**: 行业教父，将"bootstrapped SaaS"系统化为可复制方法论的布道者
- **核心身份**: MicroConf 联合创始人 / TinySeed 合伙人 / Startups For the Rest of Us 主播
- **代表作品**: *Start Small, Stay Small* (2010) / *The SaaS Playbook* (2023) / Startups For the Rest of Us podcast (700+ eps)
- **值得读/听/看的 3 件事**:
  - 📖 [The SaaS Playbook (2023)](https://robwalling.com/the-saas-playbook/) — 现代 bootstrapped SaaS 操作手册
  - 🎙️ [Startups For the Rest of Us Podcast](https://www.startupsfortherestofus.com) — 700+ 集，从创业到退出全覆盖
  - 🎬 [MicroConf Talk: Stair-Step Approach to Bootstrapping](https://microconf.com) — 楼梯法奠基演讲
- **核心思想关键词**: stair-step methodology / niche-first / default alive / bootstrapped exit / recurring revenue
- **voice_samples**:
  - [原话] "If you're going to start a software company, I want you to think about targeting a niche that has money, is reachable, and has a problem you can solve. That's really the whole game." — Startups For the Rest of Us ep. ~600, podcast_casual [T01-S002]
  - [原话] "The Stair-Step Approach says: start with a one-time sale product in a niche, get your first dollar, then step up. You de-risk every step. You're not betting everything on a SaaS from day one." — MicroConf keynote summary, conf_keynote [T01-S003]
  - [转述] 在2023年《The SaaS Playbook》中，Walling 强调 churn 才是 bootstrapped SaaS 的隐形杀手——在早期没有显著 churn 信号的阶段，创始人往往高估自己的产品市场契合度，只有当前 12 个月的留存曲线趋平才能算"真正的 PMF"。blog_long [T01-S001]
  - [原话] "Most bootstrappers I know who failed, failed because they ran out of runway — not because the market didn't exist. Default Alive is not optional; it's the whole game." — Startups For the Rest of Us, podcast_casual [T01-S002]
- **争议 / 非主流立场**: Walling 坚持 bootstrapped path 优于外部融资，但他同时创立了 TinySeed — 一个向 bootstrapped 创始人提供资金的项目。批评者认为这是内在矛盾：接受 TinySeed 资金的创始人已不再是"纯粹"的 bootstrapper。Walling 回应区别在于 TinySeed 不要求高速增长或 exit。evidence: [T01-S036]
- **最近 12 月动态**: MicroConf US 2025 举行；TinySeed batch 继续扩张；持续更新 State of Independent SaaS 年度报告 (2025 edition)。evidence: [T01-S003][T01-S036]
- **sub_skill_candidate**: true — stair-step methodology 是独立可教、可操作的框架，值得独立 skill 节点
- **可信度**: high

### 2. Pieter Levels (@levelsio)

- **One-liner**: Build-in-public 运动的精神偶像，以极简技术栈和极致透明度构建 $3M+ ARR 的 solo 帝国

- **代表作品**: NomadList (数字游民社区) / Remote OK (远程工作职位板) / PhotoAI ($100k+ MRR AI 产品)

  - 🎙️ [Lex Fridman Podcast #375 — Pieter Levels](https://lexfridman.com/pieter-levels/) — 3hr+ 深度访谈，2024年
  - 🎬 [levels.io/12-startups-12-months](https://levels.io/12-startups-12-months/) — 原始宣言文章，1.2M+ 阅读
- **核心思想关键词**: build-in-public / extreme minimalism / PHP+SQLite stack / 12 startups in 12 months / anti-framework
- **voice_samples**:
  - [原话] "I use PHP and jQuery. Everyone says I should use React or Rust or whatever. But my products work, they make money, and I can ship in days instead of weeks. The best tech stack is the one you ship with." — Twitter/X @levelsio, tweet_hot_take [T01-S031]
  - [原话] "Most people think too much before building. I just ship and see what sticks. Out of 12 startups, 2 worked. That's enough. You only need one to work." — Lex Fridman Podcast #375, podcast_casual [T01-S004]
  - [转述] Levels 在 2023-2024 年多次公开 MRR dashboard，显示 PhotoAI 从 $0 增长到 $100k+ MRR 仅用约 9 个月，主要依靠 SEO + Twitter build-in-public，无付费广告。tweet_hot_take [T01-S031]

- **可信度**: high

### 3. Arvid Kahl

- **One-liner**: 将"先找社区再造产品"的受众优先方法论系统化为独立开发者圣经的作者兼布道者
- **核心身份**: The Bootstrapped Founder 博主/作者 / FeedbackPanda 联合创始人及退出者
- **代表作品**: *Zero to Sold* (2020) / *The Embedded Entrepreneur* (2021) / The Bootstrapped Founder Newsletter (周刊)
- **值得读/听/看的 3 件事**:
  - 📖 [Zero to Sold](https://thebootstrappedfounder.com/zero-to-sold/) — 从 $0 到 $55k MRR 到退出的完整纪录
  - 📖 [The Embedded Entrepreneur](https://thebootstrappedfounder.com/the-embedded-entrepreneur/) — 受众优先方法论
  - 🎙️ [The Bootstrapped Founder Podcast](https://thebootstrappedfounder.com/podcast/) — weekly episodes + founder interviews
- **核心思想关键词**: audience-first / embedded in community / build-in-public / bootstrapped exit / FeedbackPanda
- **voice_samples**:
  - [原话] "Don't build a product and then look for an audience. Find an audience first, embed yourself in their world, understand their problems deeply — then build something they already want to pay for." — The Embedded Entrepreneur, blog_long [T01-S006]
  - [原话] "We grew FeedbackPanda to $55,000 Monthly Recurring Revenue in 18 months and then sold it. The whole time, we had no investors, no employees, just two people building for a community we loved." — Zero to Sold introduction, blog_long [T01-S007]
  - [转述] Kahl 在 2022-2023 年开始更明确地讨论"building-in-public fatigue" — 他认为 build-in-public 作为增长策略已经过于饱和，创始人应该关注社区价值而非 Twitter 关注者数量。podcast_casual [T01-S006]
  - [原话] "Your community is your distribution. If you build inside a community, you have built-in customers, built-in feedback, and built-in word-of-mouth. That's worth more than any marketing budget." — MicroConf Europe talk (转述自 newsletter), conf_keynote [T01-S003]
- **争议 / 非主流立场**: Kahl 主张创始人应该选择可退出的业务而非"lifestyle business"——这与纯粹的 bootstrapper 文化中"永不出售"的情感倾向相悖。他认为设计可退出性是理性决策，不是背叛。evidence: [T01-S006][T01-S007]
- **最近 12 月动态**: 持续发布 The Bootstrapped Founder 周刊；2024-2025 年更多聚焦 AI 工具对独立开发者的影响；举办在线付费课程。evidence: [T01-S006][T01-S032]
- **sub_skill_candidate**: true — audience-first methodology 是独立完整的方法论框架
- **可信度**: high

- **核心思想关键词**: calm company / small by choice / constraints as advantages / anti-VC / sustainable profitability
- **voice_samples**:
  - [原话] "What's our marketing strategy? We make a good product. That's not sarcasm. A great product is the best marketing you can have." — Rework, blog_long [T01-S008]
  - [原话] "Stop pretending that the only way to grow is to get big. You can run a profitable, sustainable business serving hundreds of thousands of customers without hundreds of employees or outside investment." — DHH blog, blog_long [T01-S009]

  - [原话] "VC money is a trap. It feels like a gift but comes with a hidden price: you're no longer running your business for yourself or your customers. You're running it for your investors." — DHH, various blog posts, blog_long [T01-S009]
- **争议 / 非主流立场**: 37signals 2021 年宣布禁止员工在内部讨论政治话题，引发大规模员工离职 (~1/3 员工选择离开)，损害了其"progressive workplace"形象。批评者认为"calm company"哲学被用于回避员工不满。evidence: [T01-S008][T01-S009]
- **最近 12 月动态**: 37signals 发布 ONCE 产品线 (一次性买断 SaaS)；继续 "Own Your Infrastructure" 运动；DHH 在 Twitter 持续输出反 cloud/反 VC 内容。evidence: [T01-S009]
- **sub_skill_candidate**: true — calm company + constraints philosophy 是独立可教的管理与产品哲学
- **可信度**: high
