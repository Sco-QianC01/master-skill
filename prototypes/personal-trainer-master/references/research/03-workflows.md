## Phase 2 Interface

**Common step patterns across workflows:**
- Screen/assess → design → deliver → log → review → adjust (6-step loop appears in 8/10 workflows)
- Client buy-in checkpoint before load progression in all physical workflows
- Documentation occurs at end of every session or design phase (legal + continuity)

**Beginner–Senior gap:**
- Beginners follow templates literally; seniors adapt in real-time
- Seniors collapse 3–5 beginner micro-steps into a single judgment call
- Biggest gap: onboarding (5 h → 1.5 h), program design (6 h → 45 min)

**Change triggers (when to switch workflow):**
- Client reports pain → activate Workflow 7 immediately
- Mesocycle ends → activate Workflow 5 then Workflow 2
- Attendance drops 2+ weeks → activate Workflow 10 protocol
- Client asks about nutrition → scope-of-practice check → Workflow 4

**Cold signals (workflow may be obsolete):**
- AI-generated programs replacing manual design in Workflow 2 (emerging)
- VBT replacing traditional RPE-only Workflow 8 (early adoption)
- Wearable HRV replacing coach intuition in Workflow 3 readiness check

## Workflow Cards

### 1. New Client Onboarding

**One-liner**: The structured intake-through-first-session pipeline that converts a prospect into a coached client with a documented health profile, movement baseline, and individualized program.

**Trigger**: New client signs contract or pays first invoice evidence: [T03-S009]

**Output**: Completed client file with PAR-Q+, FMS scores, baseline metrics, and first 4–8 week program delivered via platform evidence: [T03-S003]

**入门 SOP**: 1. Conduct initial consultation (goals, history, lifestyle, motivation style) 2. Administer PAR-Q+ (2025 version); stratify risk per ACSM criteria 3. Complete movement screen: FMS 7-test battery or NASM OHSA composite 4. Set SMART goals and record baselines (body comp, cardio, 1RM estimates) 5. Design first mesocycle (4–8 weeks) based on screen findings and goals 6. Orient client to delivery platform (TrueCoach/Trainerize); walk through first session 7. Conduct first supervised session; adjust program if needed; set next appointment

**Step consequences if skipped:**
- Skip PAR-Q+: legal liability, undetected contraindications; ACSM mandates risk stratification evidence: [T03-S014]
- Skip FMS: program may load dysfunctional movement patterns, accelerating injury risk evidence: [T03-S015]
- Skip baseline metrics: no objective benchmark for progress review — client perception drives retention
- Skip platform orientation: clients abandon self-directed workouts within 2 weeks (業界估) evidence: [T03-S009]

**资深路径**: 跳过 full FMS battery if obvious dysfunctions are absent (OHSA + brief clinical eye replaces 45-min FMS) / 优化 consultation to 30 min by using pre-session digital intake form (Typeform/Jotform) / 额外做 comprehensive readiness scoring via wearable HRV baseline (Oura/WHOOP) and CGM 2-week trial for body-comp clients evidence: [T03-S028]

**近期变化**: AI form-check tools (CueForm) added to initial video assessment for online clients (2025–2026) evidence: [T03-S032]; PAR-Q+ 2025 revision issued January 2025 with updated cardiovascular questions evidence: [T03-S001]

**典型耗时**: Beginner — 3–5 h across 2 sessions; Senior — 90 min total (60 min intake + 30 min design with templates)

**关键工具**: PAR-Q+ (T03-S001), FMS kit (T02-S012), TrueCoach/Trainerize (T02-S001/T02-S002), InBody (T02-S018)

**关键人物**: Gray Cook (FMS), Jonathan Goodman (PTDC onboarding systems), ACSM (risk stratification criteria)

**常见失败模式**:
1. Skipping documentation under time pressure — legally invalidates scope-of-practice protection; client injury claims trace back to missing PAR-Q+ evidence: [T03-S033]
2. Prescribing program before completing movement screen — loads compensatory patterns, produces early pain/dropout; correctable by treating FMS as non-negotiable gate evidence: [T03-S015]

**Last_updated**: 2026-05-23 | **Decay risk**: Low | **Sources**: T03-S001, T03-S003, T03-S009, T03-S014, T03-S015

### 2. Program Design (Periodized)

**One-liner**: The evidence-based process of translating client goals, training age, and movement capacity into a structured multi-week plan with defined volume, intensity, frequency, and built-in progression.

**Trigger**: New client completes onboarding OR current mesocycle ends (every 4–8 weeks) evidence: [T03-S007]

**Output**: Written 4–8 week mesocycle with exercise selection, sets/reps/load scheme, progressive overload plan, and deload week scheduled evidence: [T03-S005]

**入门 SOP**: 1. Conduct needs analysis: goal type (hypertrophy/strength/endurance/fat loss), training age, injury history, schedule constraints 2. Select periodization model: linear (novice), DUP (intermediate), block (advanced) 3. Set volume landmarks using RP MEV→MAV targets per muscle group 4. Choose exercise selection based on FMS screen findings and equipment access 5. Build progressive overload plan: define how load/reps/sets increase week-to-week 6. Schedule deload: week 4, 5, or 6 depending on client recovery capacity 7. Document and upload to delivery platform with video demos

**Step consequences if skipped:**
- Skip needs analysis: mismatch between program and goal → client dissatisfaction and dropout
- Skip periodization model selection: random stimulus → plateau after 2–3 weeks (业内共识)
- Skip volume landmark planning: chronic undertraining or overreaching without guardrails evidence: [T03-S007]
- Skip deload scheduling: accumulated fatigue masks fitness; MRV exceeded without recovery week evidence: [T03-S017]

**资深路径**: 跳过 rigid periodization labeling (DUP vs block) — applies hybrid model based on client response data / 优化 volume assignment by tracking RIR drift across the mesocycle rather than prescribing fixed MEV/MAV targets from day one / 额外做 VBT velocity profiling to automate load selection and remove subjective 1RM estimation error evidence: [T03-S019]

**近期变化**: AI programming apps (Juggernaut AI, RP Hypertrophy App, Dr. Muscle) now handle novice/intermediate program generation; senior PTs differentiate via complex periodization, sport-specificity, and coaching relationship rather than template construction evidence: [T03-S027]; 2025 meta-regression (SportRxiv) reinforces higher training frequency superiority for hypertrophy evidence: [T03-S018]

**典型耗时**: Beginner — 4–6 h (learning templates, calculating volumes); Senior — 30–60 min with established client history

**关键工具**: RP Hypertrophy App (T02-S025), Juggernaut AI (T02-S023), TrueCoach/TrainHeroic (T02-S001/T02-S003), GymAware VBT (T02-S016)

**关键人物**: Mike Israetel (RP volume landmarks), Eric Helms (hierarchy of variables), Mark Rippetoe (novice linear progression), Tudor Bompa (periodization theory)

**常见失败模式**:
1. Volume creep without deload — PT adds sets each week indefinitely; client hits MRV, gets injured or burned out; fix: pre-program deload as Week 5 or 6, not reactive evidence: [T03-S017]
2. Cookie-cutter programs across all clients — same template for 22-year-old athlete and 55-year-old sedentary adult; fix: needs analysis gates exercise selection and intensity ceiling evidence: [T03-S003]

**Last_updated**: 2026-05-23 | **Decay risk**: Low | **Sources**: T03-S003, T03-S005, T03-S007, T03-S017, T03-S018

### 3. In-Session Coaching

**One-liner**: The real-time delivery of each training session — from readiness check through movement coaching, load autoregulation, and session documentation.

**Trigger**: Client arrives at scheduled training session (in-person or live virtual) evidence: [T03-S025]

**Output**: Completed session log with loads, RPE/RIR, coaching cues used, and notes for next session evidence: [T03-S016]

**入门 SOP**: 1. Pre-session check-in: sleep quality, soreness (0–10), stress level, appetite — adjust session intensity if flagged 2. Dynamic warm-up: 5–10 min movement prep specific to main work (hip mobility before squats, etc.) 3. RPE/readiness confirmation: ask client perceived readiness; cross-reference vs wearable HRV if available 4. Main compound work: coach technique with external attentional focus cues; log actual loads and RIR each set 5. Accessory/isolation work: less coaching intensity; client often self-directs with PT oversight 6. Cool-down: 5 min light movement and breathing reset 7. Session debrief: discuss what worked, any pain signals, preview next session; update training log in platform

**Step consequences if skipped:**
- Skip readiness check: load too high on fatigued CNS → breakdown in technique → injury risk
- Skip warm-up: cold tissue under load; injury rate rises, especially hip/shoulder evidence: [T03-S005]
- Skip RIR logging: no autoregulation data; program cannot adapt to real-time performance; next-session load prescription is guesswork evidence: [T03-S016]
- Skip session debrief: pain signals go unreported; client feels undervalued → dropout evidence: [T03-S009]

**资深路径**: 跳过 scripted warm-up (senior coach reads client movement within 2 min and cues specifically vs running standard 10-min protocol) / 优化 cue selection using Wulf external-focus framework — external > internal cues for motor learning; switches cue type based on observed error pattern evidence: [T01-S019] / 额外做 real-time velocity tracking on main lifts via GymAware/FLEX to autoregulate load within sets, not just between sets evidence: [T03-S019]

**近期变化**: AI form check (CueForm, Tempo) entering in-person sessions as second-opinion overlay (2025–2026) evidence: [T03-S032]; HRV-based readiness threshold replacing coach-intuition gut-check in high-performance settings evidence: [T03-S028]; ACSM 2026 trends cite AI-assisted coaching as #3 fitness trend evidence: [T03-S026]

**典型耗时**: Beginner PT — 60–75 min/session (over-explaining, under-cueing); Senior PT — 45–60 min tightly paced

**关键工具**: Training log (TrueCoach/Trainerize), GymAware VBT (T02-S016), WHOOP/Oura HRV wearable (T02-S026/T02-S028), CueForm AI (T03-S032)

**关键人物**: Brad Schoenfeld (mechanical tension stimulus), Gabrielle Wulf (external attentional focus motor learning), Rippetoe (technique cueing hierarchy)

**常见失败模式**:
1. Internal cue overload — coaching "squeeze your glutes at the top" (internal) when the error is knee cave (which responds better to external cue "push floor apart") — fix: apply Wulf focus hierarchy; external cues outperform internal for skill acquisition evidence: [T01-S019]
2. Ignoring RPE drift — if RPE 8 set looks like RPE 5 on video, client is underestimating; unchecked underestimation produces insufficient stimulus; fix: periodically video sets and compare coach vs client RIR estimation evidence: [T03-S016]

**Last_updated**: 2026-05-23 | **Decay risk**: Low | **Sources**: T03-S009, T03-S016, T03-S025, T03-S026, T03-S028

### 4. Nutrition Guidance (Within Scope)

**One-liner**: A scope-of-practice–bounded nutrition coaching workflow that provides general caloric and macronutrient targets, tracking tools, and behavioral support without crossing into medical nutrition therapy.

**Trigger**: Client identifies body composition change or energy management as a goal, or PT identifies nutrition as limiting factor evidence: [T03-S012]

**Output**: Documented nutrition framework (calorie range, macro split, tracking tool choice) within PT scope; referral letter if clinical need identified evidence: [T03-S005]

**入门 SOP**: 1. Scope-of-practice gate: verify no medical conditions (diabetes, eating disorder history, renal disease) requiring RD/MD referral 2. Establish energy baseline: use TDEE estimate (Mifflin-St Jeor or wearable data) + activity multiplier 3. Set caloric target: deficit for fat loss (−250 to −500 kcal/day), surplus for muscle gain (+100–300 kcal/day) 4. Set macros: protein priority (1.6–2.2 g/kg for muscle retention); carb/fat split to client preference evidence: [T04-S017] 5. Recommend tracking tool: MyFitnessPal for habit formation; MacroFactor for adaptive algorithm 6. Weekly check-in: review adherence, adjust target if body weight change deviates from expectation by Week 2 7. Re-evaluate at mesocycle end; refer to RD if client presents medical/clinical complexity

**Step consequences if skipped:**
- Skip scope check: providing nutrition therapy to a client with kidney disease or active eating disorder = unauthorized medical practice evidence: [T03-S033]
- Skip protein target: muscle loss during caloric deficit undermines body composition goal; 1.6 g/kg minimum validated evidence: [T04-S017]
- Skip tracking tool recommendation: clients overestimate calorie burn, underestimate intake by 30–40% (業界研究均值); no tracking = no data evidence: [T03-S012]

**资深路径**: 跳过 manual TDEE calculation — use MacroFactor's adaptive algorithm which auto-adjusts based on actual weight trend, eliminating formula error / 优化 protein prescription to 2.2–3.1 g/kg for advanced athletes in deficit (evidence supports higher end for lean mass preservation) / 额外做 CGM 2-week trial for clients with body composition plateaus to identify glycemic patterns interfering with fat loss evidence: [T02-S029]

**近期变化**: Precision Nutrition updated PN L1 curriculum in 2025 to emphasize habit-based coaching over macro calculation (behavioral shift); CGM consumer access (Dexcom Stelo OTC) now enables non-diabetic metabolic data without prescription evidence: [T02-S029]; ACSM 2026 trends rank "personalized nutrition" #7 evidence: [T03-S026]

**典型耗时**: Beginner — 45–60 min initial consultation + 15 min/week check-in; Senior — 20–30 min initial (pre-intake form) + 5–10 min/week async

**关键工具**: MacroFactor (T02-S010), MyFitnessPal (T02-S008), Precision Nutrition curriculum (T02-S022), Cronometer (T02-S009), CGM Levels (T02-S037)

**关键人物**: Layne Norton (research translation), Precision Nutrition team (habit-based coaching), Eric Helms (Muscle & Strength Pyramids nutrition volume)

**常见失败模式**:
1. Crossing scope boundary — prescribing therapeutic diets for medical conditions without RD supervision; creates legal exposure; fix: maintain a referral list of local RDs for warm handoffs evidence: [T03-S033]
2. Calorie target without protein anchor — clients hit calorie goal eating low-protein foods; lean mass suffers in deficit; fix: set protein target first, fill remaining calories with carb/fat based on preference evidence: [T04-S017]

**Last_updated**: 2026-05-23 | **Decay risk**: Medium (CGM/AI tools evolving) | **Sources**: T03-S005, T03-S012, T03-S033, T04-S017, T03-S026

### 5. Client Progress Review

**One-liner**: The structured end-of-mesocycle evaluation that compares objective metrics against baselines, reassesses movement quality, and determines program modifications for the next phase.

**Trigger**: End of mesocycle (4–8 weeks) or client milestone event evidence: [T03-S005]

**Output**: Updated client file with re-assessed metrics, progress narrative, and next mesocycle program initiated evidence: [T03-S003]

**入门 SOP**: 1. Pull training logs: review adherence rate, load progression, session RPE trends across the mesocycle 2. Re-assess body composition: body weight trend, circumference measurements, or InBody if available 3. Re-test baseline metrics: repeat movement screen (FMS subset) and any strength benchmarks used at onboarding 4. Compare to initial baselines: quantify progress vs goal (strength %, body weight Δ, movement score Δ) 5. Identify limiting factors: what held back progress (adherence, nutrition, sleep, programming error) 6. Adjust program: update periodization phase, volume landmarks, exercise selection for next mesocycle 7. Present results to client: share visual progress chart; frame as coaching win even for partial progress

**Step consequences if skipped:**
- Skip log review: program design for next phase is uninformed; same volume/intensity errors repeat
- Skip body comp re-assessment: client relies on mirror; subjective perception drives premature program changes
- Skip movement re-screen: corrective exercise needs may have changed; loading dysfunctional patterns continues evidence: [T03-S015]
- Skip results presentation: client doesn't perceive progress → retention risk spikes evidence: [T03-S009]

**资深路径**: 跳过 full FMS re-battery (monitors target screens only — the patterns relevant to client's goals and previous limitations) / 优化 progress visualization using platform-native charts (TrueCoach/Trainerize dashboards) for client-facing report in <5 min / 额外做 muscle group-level MRV feedback analysis — identifies which muscles grew vs stagnated to refine volume distribution in next block evidence: [T03-S007]

**近期变化**: Wearable continuous tracking (Oura, WHOOP) provides between-session recovery data that supplements end-of-mesocycle review with trend graphs evidence: [T03-S028]; 3D body scanning (Styku) replacing tape measures in high-end gym settings for objective circumference deltas evidence: [T02-S019]

**典型耗时**: Beginner — 60–90 min (pulling data, writing report); Senior — 20–30 min (automated platform charts + 15 min client conversation)

**关键工具**: TrueCoach/Trainerize dashboards (T02-S001/T02-S002), InBody (T02-S018), Styku 3D scanner (T02-S019), Oura/WHOOP (T03-S028)

**关键人物**: Eric Helms (hierarchy of variables for progress review), Brad Schoenfeld (volume response tracking), Jonathan Goodman (retention through progress communication)

**常见失败模式**:
1. Progress review skipped during busy periods — client perceives stagnation; coach loses structured feedback loop; fix: block review sessions in calendar as non-negotiable 50-min appointments evidence: [T03-S009]
2. Comparing relative gains to absolute expectations — client doing Starting Strength gains 5 kg to squat but expected 20 kg; coach must set realistic expectation bands at onboarding (novice: 1–2 kg/month muscle; intermediate: 0.5–1 kg/month) evidence: [T03-S013]

**Last_updated**: 2026-05-23 | **Decay risk**: Low | **Sources**: T03-S003, T03-S007, T03-S009, T03-S013, T03-S015

### 6. Online Coaching Workflow

**One-liner**: The end-to-end remote coaching system — from digital intake through asynchronous program delivery, video form checks, and scalable weekly check-ins — that allows a PT to coach 20–200+ clients without geographic constraints.

**Trigger**: Remote client signs contract; or in-person client transitions to remote evidence: [T03-S009]

**Output**: Running online coaching relationship with documented program, asynchronous check-in cadence, and clear video submission protocol evidence: [T03-S010]

**入门 SOP**: 1. Digital intake: send pre-built form (Typeform/Jotform) covering health history, goals, training age, equipment access, schedule — replaces in-person consultation 2. Video movement assessment: request 5–7 self-filmed movement videos (squat, hinge, push, pull, lunge) before program design 3. Program build: design and upload to platform (TrueCoach/Trainerize/PT Distinction) with annotated exercise videos 4. Onboarding call: 30-min Zoom to orient client to platform, set expectations for check-in cadence, establish video submission guidelines 5. Weekly check-in: async voice note or written check-in covering training adherence, soreness, nutrition, life stress 6. Video form review: client submits 1–2 form videos/week; PT responds with timestamped cue corrections 7. Biweekly program adjustment: update loads, swap exercises, or add volume based on check-in data

**Step consequences if skipped:**
- Skip video assessment: program may not match client mobility/equipment reality; first 2 weeks produce injury or frustration
- Skip platform orientation call: clients do not use the app consistently; abandonment within 7 days (業界估) evidence: [T03-S010]
- Skip weekly check-in: PT loses early warning of overtraining, life disruption, or waning motivation
- Skip video form review: technique errors compound; client perceives coach as absent → churn evidence: [T03-S009]

**资深路径**: 跳过 initial Zoom call for clients with clear intake data — delivers loom video walkthrough instead, saving 30 min/client while maintaining personal feel / 优化 check-in system with templated question framework (sleep/training/nutrition/mindset 4-quadrant) to extract signal efficiently / 额外做 AI form check tool (CueForm) as triage layer — flags high-confidence errors before PT watches video, prioritizing PT review time evidence: [T03-S032]

**近期变化**: TrueCoach added 5% transaction fee January 2026; PTs migrating to PT Distinction for margin reasons evidence: [T02-S004]; AI form analysis tools entering online coaching stack (2025) making form review faster and more scalable; Keep (China) growing PT coaching feature for zh-CN market evidence: [T02-S033]

**典型耗时**: Beginner — 3–4 h setup per client + 2–3 h/week for 10 clients; Senior — 60–90 min setup + 30–45 min/week for 30+ clients (batched check-ins)

**关键工具**: TrueCoach (T02-S001), PT Distinction (T02-S004), ABC Trainerize (T02-S002), CueForm AI (T03-S032), Calendly (T02-S036)

**关键人物**: Jonathan Goodman (PTDC online trainer methodology), TrueCoach/Trainerize platform teams

**常见失败模式**:
1. No video form feedback policy — clients feel unseen; PT doesn't know if technique is degenerating; fix: make 1 form video/week mandatory in contract, build review into weekly workflow evidence: [T03-S009]
2. Platform overload — coaching on WhatsApp + Google Sheets + Calendly + email; clients confused, PT overwhelmed; fix: consolidate to one platform with built-in messaging, programming, and payment evidence: [T03-S010]

### 7. Injury/Pain Management Referral

**One-liner**: The protocol for identifying red-flag pain signals, pausing or modifying training, coordinating with healthcare providers, and safely returning the client to full programming.

**Trigger**: Client reports new or worsening pain, unusual movement limitation, or ACSM red-flag symptom during pre-session check-in evidence: [T03-S005]

**Output**: Documented referral to appropriate provider + modified program around limitation + return-to-training clearance letter from provider evidence: [T03-S008]

**入门 SOP**: 1. Red-flag triage: identify ACSM absolute contraindications (chest pain, dizziness, dyspnea at rest, recent cardiac event) — if present, stop session, call 911 or refer to ED 2. Relative contraindication check: musculoskeletal pain >3/10 NRS at rest, or mechanism of injury (traumatic vs insidious onset) — document precisely 3. Scope-of-practice decision: PT does not diagnose; if pain is musculoskeletal and non-emergency, refer to Sports PT/orthopedic MD with documented symptom description 4. Interim program modification: remove painful movement pattern; substitute pain-free variations that maintain training stimulus 5. Maintain contact with client: continue coaching around limitation to preserve relationship and momentum 6. Receive clearance letter or return-to-activity note from provider: document in client file 7. Gradual reintroduction: return to full program in progressive loading phases (BFR/light load → moderate → full load) over 2–4 weeks

**Step consequences if skipped:**
- Skip red-flag triage: PT continues training client with undetected cardiovascular event → medical emergency liability evidence: [T03-S005]
- Skip referral: PT attempts to treat pain beyond scope; legal exposure (practicing medicine without license) evidence: [T03-S033]
- Skip program modification: forcing client to rest entirely → detraining + dropout; evidence supports training around injury evidence: [T03-S008]
- Skip clearance: resuming full load without provider sign-off re-injures client; liability chain evidence: [T03-S033]

**资深路径**: 跳过 generic "rest and refer" response — uses BFR training (SmartCuffs) to maintain muscle mass at very low loads (20–30% 1RM) during recovery period, preserving fitness while injured evidence: [T02-S017] / 优化 provider communication with structured referral note format (presenting complaint, session history, PT clinical impression, specific return-to-training questions) for faster clearance / 额外做 functional outcome tracking — OSPRO-YF or pain NRS + movement score at each session to document return-to-function trajectory for medicolegal record evidence: [T03-S008]

**近期变化**: Barbell Medicine (Feigenbaum/Baraki) popularized pain neuroscience education for PTs — teaching clients pain ≠ damage reduces catastrophizing and dropout during injury management (2023–2025) evidence: [T03-S008]; SmartCuffs 4.0 BFR mainstream adoption in gym settings since 2024 evidence: [T02-S017]

**典型耗时**: Beginner — 30–60 min per incident (documenting, finding referral, modifying plan); Senior — 15–20 min (established referral network, template referral letter, BFR protocol ready)

**关键工具**: SmartCuffs BFR (T02-S017), TrueCoach session notes (T02-S001), ACSM contraindication checklist (T03-S005)

**关键人物**: Jordan Feigenbaum & Austin Baraki (clinical strength medicine, Barbell Medicine), ACSM (contraindication criteria), Gray Cook (FMS corrective hierarchy)

**常见失败模式**:
1. Binary rest/refer with no interim program — client stops training entirely → detraining begins in day 3–5; relationship ruptures; fix: always prescribe modified training around the injury while awaiting clearance evidence: [T03-S008]
2. Documenting pain as vague "knee bothers client" — inadequate for medicolegal record; fix: document location, NRS intensity, mechanism, onset date, aggravating/relieving factors at every session mention evidence: [T03-S033]

**Last_updated**: 2026-05-23 | **Decay risk**: Low | **Sources**: T03-S005, T03-S008, T03-S033

### 8. VBT-Based Autoregulation

**One-liner**: A data-driven in-session load management workflow using velocity-based training technology to precisely prescribe, monitor, and adjust resistance based on bar speed — replacing subjective RPE estimation with objective barbell velocity.

**Trigger**: Client is intermediate-to-advanced strength trainee; or PT adopts VBT device (GymAware, FLEX, RepOne) evidence: [T03-S019]

**Output**: Per-session load prescription and fatigue management based on mean concentric velocity (MCV) targets, with documented velocity loss thresholds evidence: [T03-S019]

**入门 SOP**: 1. Build client velocity profile: test MCV at multiple loads across 40–90% 1RM to create individualized load-velocity relationship (LVR) curve 2. Set velocity zone targets: explosive (>1.0 m/s), strength-speed (0.75–1.0 m/s), strength (0.5–0.75 m/s), maximal strength (<0.5 m/s) 3. Intra-session autoregulation: start each set at prescribed zone; if MCV drops >10% from best set velocity, end set (velocity stop criterion) 4. Day-to-day autoregulation: if session-opening velocity is >5% below profile at prescribed % load, reduce load to match target zone — client is fatigued 5. Volume autoregulation: monitor cumulative velocity loss across all sets; stop exercise when total velocity loss hits 20% threshold (hypertrophy) or 10% (strength focus) 6. Log MCV per set: GymAware/FLEX/RepOne auto-records; review trend data weekly 7. Update LVR profile: re-test every 4–6 weeks or after deload to account for strength gains

**Step consequences if skipped:**
- Skip LVR profiling: velocity targets are generic, not individualized — loses primary advantage of VBT
- Skip velocity stop criterion: clients grind through fatigue producing degraded technique reps; VBT becomes redundant evidence: [T03-S019]
- Skip day-to-day readiness check via velocity: misses real-time fatigue — PT would use subjective RPE alone
- Skip periodic LVR update: profile becomes stale as strength improves; targets drift out of intended zone

**资深路径**: 跳过 full LVR profiling for experienced clients — uses Jovanovic/Mann reference tables (published load-velocity norms per exercise) as starting point, calibrates after 2 sessions / 优化 velocity loss threshold by training block: 10% loss for strength blocks, 20–30% for hypertrophy blocks, 5% for power (research-backed thresholds) / 额外做 force-velocity profiling (sprint or jump assessment) to identify whether client is force-deficient or velocity-deficient and weight program accordingly evidence: [T03-S019]

**近期变化**: FLEX (GymAware) 2024 model adds cloud dashboard for multi-client fleet management; RepOne adds AI load recommendations (2025); VBT now #9 ACSM 2026 fitness trend evidence: [T03-S026]; Consumer-grade accelerometers (PUSH Band, Vmaxpro) now under $300 making VBT accessible for independent PTs evidence: [T03-S019]

**典型耗时**: Beginner — 30–45 min added per session during LVR profiling phase; Senior PT with established profiles — 5–10 min additional cognitive load per session (device auto-logs data)

**关键工具**: GymAware / FLEX (T02-S016), RepOne, PUSH Band — linked T02-S019 VBT buyer's guide

**关键人物**: Mike Israetel (volume autoregulation framework), GymAware team (device innovation), Bryan Mann (VBT research pioneer)

**常见失败模式**:
1. Using VBT as monitoring-only without acting on velocity data — attaches device but programs loads the same as before; velocity is ignored; fix: pre-commit to velocity stop criterion before session; VBT only adds value if autoregulation decisions are pre-defined evidence: [T03-S019]
2. Generic LVR without individualization — squat LVR from population norms applied to client with unusual anthropometry; load prescription off by 10–20%; fix: always profile the individual with minimum 4–5 test loads evidence: [T03-S019]

**Last_updated**: 2026-05-23 | **Decay risk**: High (hardware/software evolving rapidly) | **Sources**: T03-S019, T03-S026

### 9. Continuing Education & Recertification

**One-liner**: The biennial workflow for fulfilling CEU requirements, selecting specialty certifications, documenting competency expansion, and renewing primary credentials — while keeping up with evidence evolution.

**Trigger**: CEU deadline approaching (typically every 2 years per cert body); or PT identifies skill gap limiting client outcomes evidence: [T03-S020]

**Output**: Renewed primary certification + documented CEU log + ideally one specialty credential (CES, PES, SFN, PN L1) adding service line evidence: [T03-S021]

**入门 SOP**: 1. Audit CEU requirements: NASM (2.0 CEUs/2yr), NSCA (6.0 CECs/3yr), ACSM (30 credits/3yr), ACE (2.0 CECs/2yr) — check current cycle deadline 2. Identify skill gap: survey current client base — what populations or goals cannot be served? (older adults, sport performance, nutrition, pre/postnatal) 3. Select specialty: NASM CES (corrective), NASM PES (performance), ACSM SFN (nutrition), PN L1 (coaching), StrongFirst SFG (kettlebell) 4. Complete coursework: online modules + practical (if required); allow 40–80 h for major specialty 5. Attend 1 live event/year: NSCA National, IDEA World, Perform Better Summit — for hands-on labs, networking, mentorship evidence: [T05-S017] 6. Apply new techniques to 1–2 current clients first; document outcomes 7. Submit CEU documentation to cert body; renew; update LinkedIn/bio with new credential

**Step consequences if skipped:**
- Skip CEU audit: certification lapses; PT cannot legally present as certified; insurance invalidated evidence: [T03-S024]
- Skip specialty identification: service offering stays narrow; client referrals missed
- Skip live event attendance: purely online CE misses tactile skill refinement; corrective/movement PTs especially need hands-on hours
- Skip applying new techniques: CEU becomes credential theater, not competency evidence: [T03-S021]

**资深路径**: 跳过 broad CEU collection — focuses all CEUs in one specialty per cycle to build genuine depth rather than scattered breadcrumbs / 优化 evidence currency by subscribing to MASS Research Review monthly — counts as 1.0 CEU/yr with NASM/NSCA while providing field-leading research evidence: [T03-S018] / 额外做 mentorship relationship (present to mentor for case review 2x/year) which accelerates skill transfer faster than any CEU course evidence: [T05-S019]

**近期变化**: China 全民健身条例 (2025 revision draft) shifts health fitness instructor credential toward new tiered exam — BISM Level 1/2/3 replacing legacy SRA certification (2024–2026 transition) evidence: [T03-S023]; ACSM adds AI literacy module to 2025 CEU catalog evidence: [T03-S026]; NSCA introduces online practical assessment option for CSCS recertification 2025 evidence: [T03-S020]

**典型耗时**: Beginner — 20–40 h/2-year cycle (scrambling at deadline); Senior — 10–15 h/year (ongoing subscription reading + 1 conference/year)

**关键工具**: NASM CE portal, NSCA CEC tracker, MASS Research Review (T03-S018), IDEA World conference (T05-S019)

**关键人物**: NSCA, NASM, ACSM, ACE (cert bodies); Menno Henselmans (PT education courses); Eric Helms/MASS team (ongoing evidence synthesis)

**常见失败模式**:
1. CEU panic at renewal deadline — bulk purchasing low-quality webinars 30 days before expiry; CE learned under pressure with no application; fix: distribute 3–4 CEUs/year as annual Q1 planning task evidence: [T03-S024]
2. Accumulating credentials without deepening any — 5 certificates, none mastered; clients cannot tell difference; fix: commit to one specialty for 2 years before adding the next evidence: [T03-S021]

### 10. Business Development (Independent PT)

**One-liner**: The structured pathway from credentialed employee PT to independent or online PT business — covering liability protection, pricing, lead generation, client retention, and scalable revenue model.

**Trigger**: PT wants to leave gym employment, go independent, or launch online coaching as primary or supplemental income evidence: [T03-S009]

**Output**: Operating independent PT business with liability insurance, pricing structure, recurring revenue ≥ breakeven, and documented systems for lead generation and retention evidence: [T03-S009]

**入门 SOP**: 1. Legal foundation: obtain liability insurance ($2M/$4M min policy; IDEA, Insure Fitness, or NASM-affiliated carrier) + LLC or sole proprietor registration 2. Certification compliance: verify primary cert is in good standing; add PN L1 or NASM specialty to justify premium pricing evidence: [T03-S024] 3. Pricing model: set session-rate or package pricing (market rate: $60–$150/session in US urban; $100–$500/month online) — cost-plus model including insurance, platform, CEU 4. Lead generation: define ideal client avatar; build 3-channel referral system (current client referrals, local medical referrals, organic content on one platform) 5. Consultation process: free 30-min discovery call → assessment session → program proposal → close; use Goodman's 3-step framework: connect → identify → solve evidence: [T03-S009] 6. Retention strategy: milestone celebrations, progress reviews (Workflow 5), re-assessment offers every 8 weeks 7. Scaling: when client roster hits 80%+ capacity, introduce online hybrid model or raise rates — avoid adding time before raising price

**Step consequences if skipped:**
- Skip liability insurance: single client injury lawsuit can exceed personal net worth; $2M policy costs ~$200/year evidence: [T03-S024]
- Skip pricing structure: underpricing leads to burnout at 40 sessions/week; proper pricing is the first business sustainability lever
- Skip referral system: relying on gym walk-ins → zero pipeline when you go independent; referrals take 3–6 months to build
- Skip retention strategy: average client stays 3–6 months without active retention; $0 revenue from churn evidence: [T03-S009]

**资深路径**: 跳过 gradual rate increases — sets premium positioning from day one (no discount starter clients who anchor at low rate) / 优化 lead generation to one platform done deeply (email list or YouTube or Instagram) vs scattered presence on four / 额外做 group coaching hybrid: run monthly paid workshop or online group program for past clients; converts 1:1 time into 1:many revenue without full productization complexity evidence: [T03-S009]

**近期变化**: TrueCoach 5% transaction fee (Jan 2026) reduced net per session for platform-dependent coaches — PTs moving to Stripe-direct billing via PT Distinction evidence: [T02-S004]; China market: Keep Pro PT coaching network growing; 2025 first profitable year for Keep; urban PT rates in Tier 1 cities reaching 500–800 CNY/session (上海/北京 业界均值) evidence: [T02-S033]; PTDC 2025 survey: online coaching surpassed in-person for new PT income within first 2 years of independence evidence: [T03-S009]

**典型耗时**: Beginner — 6–12 months to reach sustainable client roster; Senior (transitioning from employed) — 1–3 months to port existing client base

**关键工具**: PT Distinction/TrueCoach (T02-S001/T02-S004), Calendly/Acuity (T02-S011), Stripe (payment), Insure Fitness insurance (T03-S024)

**关键人物**: Jonathan Goodman (PTDC — definitive online PT business framework), PTDC/The Online Trainer Show

**常见失败模式**:
1. Launching online coaching without an existing client base or audience — starting from zero; fix: keep 5–10 in-person clients as income anchor while building online pipeline over 6 months before fully transitioning evidence: [T03-S009]
2. Session-by-session pricing with no packages — revenue unpredictable; client commitment low; fix: sell 8- or 12-session packages or monthly retainers; upfront commitment improves retention and cash flow evidence: [T03-S009]
